Answer in one paragraph

The research does not support the edition's blanket claim that Benjamin's German essay is simply in the public domain. The authorial term has expired in Germany and some other jurisdictions, but the prudent United States assumption is that the essay remains protected through 31 December 2050. The exact 1955 publication day and the absence of US publication within the following thirty days remain unproved; neither gap is evidence that the work is free to use. Suhrkamp is the strongest first clearance administrator for the German source and a new English translation, although public records do not prove the ultimate ownership chain.

What the documentary record establishes

Proven Lifetime nonpublication and controlled circulation

Benjamin composed the essay between 4 and 11 November 1916, delivered it to Gershom Scholem in December, and later circulated manuscript copies among a small named group under restrictions. The critical-edition report and Harvard's editorial note agree that it remained unpublished in Benjamin's lifetime.

Proven First documented print publication

The first located publisher-issued printing is in Walter Benjamin, Schriften, vol. II, ed. Theodor W. Adorno and Gretel Adorno with Friedrich Podszus (Frankfurt am Main: Suhrkamp, 1955), pp. 401–419. The German National Library's original contents scan establishes volume II and the page range. An earlier agent's statement that the essay appeared in volume I was incorrect.

Strong evidence Mid-October 1955 release

National-library and union-catalog records prove the year, place, publisher, and extent. A scholarly reconstruction from publisher and Adorno–Scholem correspondence dates the two-volume set to mid-October 1955. No exact calendar day was established.

Open fact US publication in the following thirty days

No US edition, co-imprint, or qualifying US publication was found. US library records describe the Frankfurt edition, and searches of both halves of the official 1955 Catalog of Copyright Entries found no Benjamin–Suhrkamp Schriften entry. These are meaningful negative results, not proof: registration and publication are different facts, and no publisher shipping ledger or complete contemporaneous US distribution record was obtained.

The United States working conclusion

On the best-supported facts, the edition should act as though the underlying essay remains protected in the United States through 31 December 2050 and enters the US public domain on 1 January 2051.

Why a work written in 1916 can still be protected

The essay's age is not being ignored; two different dates are doing different legal work. Benjamin wrote the manuscript in 1916 but did not publish it. For this class of pre-1978 work, the possible United States term is calculated from the first publication in 1955. The URAA later restored qualifying foreign copyrights for the remainder of the term they would have received had they never entered the US public domain. The resulting working calculation is therefore 1955 + 95 years, carried to the end of 2050—not 1916 + 95 years. Germany uses a different ordinary rule based on the author's death, which is why the German term expired after 2010 while the possible US term continues.

Revised resolution options

A. Withdraw complete texts pending clearance

Temporarily remove the complete German and English reading texts while retaining the project description, commentary supported by limited quotations, research record, and rights notice.

Tradeoff: legally safest; most disruptive to the edition.

B. Correct, narrow, and keep online

Immediately correct the territorial claims and narrow CC BY, while leaving the complete edition accessible during an expedited clearance process.

Tradeoff: truthful and continuous, but corrected wording does not cure any missing permission.

C. Seek a durable written license

Ask Suhrkamp to confirm its capacity and grant worldwide rights for the German text, the new translation, living revisions, repository distribution, archiving, and—if available—CC BY downstream reuse.

Tradeoff: best durable route; may cost money, impose restrictions, take time, or be refused.

D. Rely on a counsel-reviewed legal position

Commission a targeted title/fair-use opinion after completing the remaining records search, then publish within the scope counsel considers supportable.

Tradeoff: may support publication without a license, but requires professional analysis and may still recommend withdrawal or clearance.

Fresh recommendation

Adopt B + C now, with D as the closing condition. This respects the editor's previously chosen interim posture—continued access during research—while correcting the public representation and pursuing the only clearly evidenced permission route. Option A remains the legally safest alternative and should be reconsidered if the administrator objects, clearance stalls, or counsel advises withdrawal.

  1. Adopt US protection through 2050 as the project's working assumption, not as a final legal adjudication.
  2. Publish a prompt accuracy correction across README, LICENSE, bibliography, colophon, and metadata.
  3. Pending clearance, exclude the German text, this complete English translation, Jephcott quotations, and all other third-party material from CC BY.
  4. Prepare a coordinated Suhrkamp request, but do not send it until the editor approves the exact scope, willingness to pay, and fallback if CC BY is unavailable.
  5. Preserve CC BY for commentary, annotations, epilegomena, and original page design. Treat CC BY reuse of the complete translation as negotiable rather than a condition of keeping the project alive.
  6. Inventory every Jephcott quotation by page and word count. Decide separately, with counsel if needed, whether to continue under fair use, reduce quotations, or request permission from HarperCollins.
  7. Do not mark AUD-P0-001 resolved until a written grant or counsel-reviewed alternative establishes the publication basis and every public surface agrees.

Permission request: scope to approve before contact

The proposed Suhrkamp request would identify the 1916 essay, its 1955 Schriften publication, the 1977 Gesammelte Schriften witness, the current public URL, and the GitHub repository. It would request:

  • non-exclusive worldwide reproduction, distribution, public display, and making-available rights for the complete German text;
  • the right to prepare, publish, revise, and archive this complete new English translation;
  • parallel German/English presentation, sentence-level quotation in commentary, linking, indexing, and ordinary technical reformatting;
  • living-edition corrections, permanent version history, public repository copies, browser caching, citation, and noncommercial archival preservation;
  • free public access without advertising, subscription, or sale of the text;
  • an express decision on whether readers may reuse the translation under CC BY 4.0;
  • the licensor's exact legal capacity, principal, territorial/media exclusions, required credit, fee, approval rights, and whether the grant ratifies publication before permission.

No outreach is authorized by this page. Contacting Suhrkamp, HarperCollins, HUP, the Archive, or counsel is a separate external action requiring the editor's approval.

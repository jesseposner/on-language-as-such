The opening problem

The current local edition inserts a compact “Translation term · Quintessence” notice immediately before ¶1. The full translator’s note later provides the complete policy, while the reading text itself repeats the quintessence family 61 times. At the same time, the Revision History is technically linked from the footer and Sources, but not discoverable near the title.

Superseding editorial decision: remove the standalone opening notice, retain the full translator’s note, and use the newly available opening space for a compact “Living edition · Revision history” link beneath the byline.

Why this is the cleaner opening

  • The translation should begin with Benjamin’s first paragraph rather than an editorial warning.
  • The technical use is established by repetition and remains fully disclosed in the translator’s note.
  • The Revision History is structural information about the edition and should be visible before a reader begins.
  • Placing the link beneath the byline avoids adding another button to the already crowded mobile controls.

Exact reader-facing change

Remove before ¶1

“Translation term · Quintessence” and its explanatory paragraph.

Add beneath the byline

Living edition · Revision history

The new link targets #revisions through the existing select(29) navigation. Paragraph-level navigation to ¶1 will again target #p1 directly.

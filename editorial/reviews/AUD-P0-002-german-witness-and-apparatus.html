The question before the edition

The edition calls its German text both "settled" and still awaiting collation against Gesammelte Schriften II.1. The initial print check found fifteen divergences, including two lost words, a wrong biblical citation, malformed German, and several punctuation defects. Because some current readings are silent improvements on the printed text, resolution requires an explicit base-text and emendation policy—not simply a list of corrections.

This initial review was superseded at the reading level. The commissioned research found a sixteenth ED/GS divergence, GS editorial conjectures, marked German forms, and a missing emphasis layer. The fresh findings and recommendations governed the resolution published in Edition 2026.07.12.5.

Witnesses examined

  1. GS: Walter Benjamin, "Über Sprache überhaupt und über die Sprache des Menschen," in Gesammelte Schriften II.1, ed. Rolf Tiedemann and Hermann Schweppenhäuser (Suhrkamp, 1977), pp. 140–157. This is the proposed base witness.
  2. TL: the current textlog.de transcription. It contains numerous evident electronic corruptions, some corrected in the edition and some retained.
  3. ED: the German currently displayed in index.html.
  4. Unidentified online witness: mentioned in the README but not named or preserved. Its evidentiary value cannot presently be assessed.

The GS PDF is a scanned volume with imperfect OCR. The readings below were checked against the page images, not accepted from OCR alone. This review does not claim to have collated the surviving manuscript or the 1955 first edition.

Three different editorial operations

  1. Transcription: reproduce the chosen base witness accurately.
  2. Emendation: depart from the base because it appears corrupt, preserving both readings and the reason.
  3. Display normalization: regularize typography or citation style without claiming Benjamin wrote the normalized form.

The current edition combines all three without labels. A critical reading text may legitimately do so, but only if its apparatus makes the operations reversible.

Initial GS II.1 collation

Fourteen proposed actions restore the GS reading. Number 10 is different: the edition's current Name silently corrects the printed Namen and should be retained only as a declared emendation.

  1. ¶1 · GS p. 141

    Substantive omission
    ED / TLseinen Inhalt mitzuteilen
    GSseinen geistigen Inhalt mitzuteilen

    ED loses the adjective governing the essay's central vocabulary, and the English likewise says only "its content."

    Proposed: restore geistigen; revise the corresponding English under the edition's eventual geistiges Wesen policy.

  2. ¶1 · GS p. 141

    Punctuation
    ED / TList eine Idee: aber
    GSist eine Idee; aber

    Proposed: adopt the GS semicolon.

  3. ¶2 · GS p. 141

    Silent emendation
    EDund über ihm, gerade über ihm sich schwebend zu erhalten, ist ihre Aufgabe
    GS / TLund über, gerade über ihm sich schwebend zu erhalten ist ihre Aufgabe

    ED regularizes Benjamin's suspended construction by supplying a first ihm and another comma, but does not disclose the intervention.

    Proposed: restore GS in the German reading text; retain the clarifying English syntax.

  4. ¶3 · GS p. 142

    Sentence boundary
    ED / TLsprachlichen Wesen: Das geistige Wesen
    GSsprachlichen Wesen. Das geistige Wesen

    Proposed: adopt the GS full stop.

  5. ¶4 · GS p. 142

    Omitted reflexive
    ED / TLjede Sprache teilt sich in selbst mit
    GSjede Sprache teilt sich in sich selbst mit

    The first sich belongs to teilt sich mit; the second belongs to in sich selbst. The English silently gives the intended reading.

    Proposed: restore the second reflexive from GS.

  6. ¶5 · GS p. 143

    Punctuation
    ED / TLdaß er die Dinge benennt.
    GSdaß er die Dinge benennt:

    The colon opens the following question, Wozu benennt?

    Proposed: adopt the GS colon.

  7. ¶11 · GS p. 146

    Spurious comma
    ED / TLzwischen Geist, und Sprache
    GSzwischen Geist und Sprache

    Proposed: remove the comma.

  8. ¶11 · GS p. 146

    Malformed German
    ED / TLder sprachlich existentester. d. h. fixierteste Ausdruck
    GSder sprachlich existenteste, d. h. fixierteste Ausdruck

    ED has the wrong adjectival ending and breaks the apposition into a fragment.

    Proposed: adopt the GS morphology and comma.

  9. ¶14 · GS p. 148

    Wrong citation
    ED(Genesis 1:3; 1:11)
    GS / TL(1,3; 1,14)

    Genesis 1:14 is the creation command for the lights and contains the relevant Es werde. The current English and commentary repeat 1:11.

    Proposed: display (Genesis 1:3; 1:14); correct every English and commentary occurrence.

  10. ¶16 · GS p. 149

    Proposed emendation
    EDder menschliche Name
    GS / TLder menschliche Namen

    Standard nominative German requires Name. ED's reading is almost certainly right as a reading text, but it silently departs from the proposed base witness.

    Proposed: retain Name and record GS: Namen; ED em. in the public apparatus.

  11. ¶16 · GS p. 150

    Punctuation
    ED / TLAuf die Sprache der Dinge selbst; aus denen
    GSAuf die Sprache der Dinge selbst, aus denen

    Proposed: adopt the GS comma.

  12. ¶18 · GS p. 151

    Missing full stop
    ED / TLin den Namen Das ist also
    GSin den Namen. Das ist also

    The commentary's quoted German already supplies the full stop, so the edition is internally inconsistent.

    Proposed: restore the GS full stop everywhere.

  13. ¶19 · GS p. 154

    Stray quotation mark
    ED / TLDiese Unmittelbarkeit »in der Mitteilung
    GSDiese Unmittelbarkeit in der Mitteilung

    Proposed: remove the guillemet.

  14. ¶21 · GS p. 155

    Corrupt boundary
    ED / TLsie hervor-, In der Sprache
    GSsie hervor. In der Sprache

    Proposed: adopt the GS full stop and remove the stray hyphen/comma.

  15. ¶22 · GS p. 156

    Transcription error
    ED / TLeine Obersetzung der Sprache der Dinge
    GSeine Übersetzung der Sprache der Dinge

    Proposed: restore Übersetzung.

Consequences beyond the German display

  • ¶1: restoring geistigen requires an English correction. Under the current policy, "its content" would minimally become "its quintessential content." The governing translation policy is reviewed separately in AUD-P1-001.
  • ¶14: Genesis 1:11 must become 1:14 in the English translation, German display, commentary quotation, and any sentence-level discussion.
  • Quoted German: commentary blockquotes sometimes reproduce the corrupt display and sometimes silently correct it. Every duplicate must be synchronized with the adopted reading.
  • Documentation: the claims "settled," "fourteen," "fifteen," "documented," and "pending" must be replaced by one consistent account.
  • Edition history: these changes require a public Revision History entry even when the English meaning was already correct.

Proposed editorial policy

The German reading text is based on Gesammelte Schriften II.1, pp. 140–157. It follows that witness in wording, spelling, and punctuation except where an explicitly recorded emendation is adopted. Typographic normalization for web display is documented separately and does not silently alter wording or syntax.

Under this policy, fourteen current divergences return to GS. The reading text retains Name at ¶16 as one declared emendation of GS Namen. The prior textlog collation remains useful as provenance but no longer functions as the textual authority.

Resolution options

A. Diplomatic GS transcription

Display GS exactly, including the apparent der menschliche Namen error, and annotate it with sic.

Tradeoff: maximally faithful to one witness, but makes an evident error part of the reader-facing text and understates the edition's interpretive role.

B. GS-based critical reading text

Use GS as the base, adopt the fourteen GS restorations, retain Name as a declared emendation, and publish a compact apparatus.

Tradeoff: produces the most readable and accountable edition, while requiring explicit maintenance of base and reading layers.

C. Continue the textlog-derived eclectic text

Patch only obvious defects in the current HTML and document the patch list after the fact.

Tradeoff: smallest immediate code change, but retains the provenance problem and makes future collation harder. Not recommended.

Provisional recommendation

Adopt option B: a GS-based critical reading text with one declared emendation.

  1. Create the four accountable source/apparatus files before changing the display.
  2. Encode GS exactly in the diplomatic source.
  3. Apply the fourteen GS restorations to the reading text and preserve Name as the single declared departure identified by this collation.
  4. Correct the two direct English consequences at ¶1 and ¶14, with ¶1 phrased provisionally under the current translation policy.
  5. Synchronize every German quotation and update all provenance claims.
  6. Publish a concise textual note and apparatus on the site, then log the revision as a new calendar edition.

Scope boundary: this decision does not settle "quintessence," Sache, or the other local translation questions. They remain separate reviews.

Research commission · 2026-07-12

At this stage the edition framework had been approved, but no individual reading was yet authorized. Three parallel research passes were commissioned before the final textual decision:

  1. Variants 1–7: verify ¶¶1–11 against GS page images, textlog, the 1955 Schriften where obtainable, reputable later German editions, translations, and exact quotations in the edition's bibliography; sweep the same paragraphs for missed variants.
  2. Variants 8–15: perform the same work for ¶¶11–26, including the Genesis citation and the grammatical/historical status of der menschliche Namen.
  3. Adversarial full-text sweep: independently recollate the entire essay, test the assumed witness genealogy, and search for any additional emendation the first two passes missed.

Each pass distinguished visual inspection from OCR, reported conflicting evidence rather than flattening it, and assigned confidence levels to its recommendations.

Completed deliverable: AUD-P0-002A-collation-research-findings.html synthesizes the evidence and presents a fresh recommendation for every reading. The supporting records are the variants 1–7 memo, variants 8–15 memo, and adversarial full-text recollation. This original review remains the record of the initial audit and approved framework.

Acceptance criteria

AUD-P0-002 can be marked resolved only when:

  • the base witness and editorial policy are explicit;
  • all sixteen ED/GS divergences and the additional T1/GS decision have an approved disposition;
  • the diplomatic source, reading text, apparatus, and collation record exist;
  • German display and repeated German quotations agree with the approved reading;
  • the ¶1 and ¶14 English consequences are corrected;
  • README, bibliography, epilogue, colophon, and textual note report the same provenance and counts;
  • the public apparatus exposes every departure from the declared base;
  • automated or mechanical comparison confirms that the maintained reading source matches the output;
  • Revision History records the textual changes and the deployed edition is verified.

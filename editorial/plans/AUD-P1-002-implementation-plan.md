# AUD-P1-002 implementation plan

**Prepared:** 2026-07-14<br>
**Public baseline:** Edition 2026.07.12.5<br>
**Decision:** recommended package approved 2026-07-14<br>
**Implementation state:** published and resolved in Edition 2026.07.14

## Governing decision

Apply the definite local corrections, use reception-led English at ¶16–17,
render ¶1.9 with the default grammatical agency while disclosing the competing
parse, and adopt controlled estrangement as the edition's sentence-level
register policy.

One approved row was reopened during the pre-edit source check. The review
abbreviated ¶15 to `im Menschen ließ Gott die Sprache … frei`, but the
maintained GS reading is `im Menschen entließ Gott die Sprache … frei aus
sich`. Because `aus sich` is present, the review's claim that “out of himself”
was added by the current English is not sustainable. Amended option B was
approved on 2026-07-14 and preserves both vectors.

## Reading-text changes

Stable paragraph and unit identifiers remain unchanged.

### Meaning-bearing readings

| Unit | Published wording | Approved local wording | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ¶1.9 (`u1-9`) | “whose circumference traces the idea of God” | “whose circumference designates the idea of God” | Preserve the default grammatical agency and translate `bezeichnet` directly; disclose the reversible case grammar in commentary. |
| ¶12.1 (`u12-1`) | “This sentence has a double sense, according to its figurative and its literal meaning…” | “This sentence has a double sense—figurative and sensory: the languages of things are imperfect, and they are mute.” | `sinnlich` is sensory, not literal. |
| ¶15.3 (`u15-3`) | “in man, God released language … freely out of himself” | “in man God let language … go free from himself” | Preserve both `im Menschen` and `aus sich`; make `frei` describe language's freedom rather than God's manner of action. |
| ¶16.5 (`u16-5`) | “By it, each man is vouched his creation by God” | “Through it, every person's creation by God is vouched for.” | Make “creation” the grammatical subject and preserve the `vouch` family. |
| ¶16.9 (`u16-9`) | “the rejection … by the mystical theory of language is likewise open to misunderstanding” | “the mystical theory's rejection of the bourgeois theory of language also rests on a misunderstanding” | Benjamin identifies the mystical theory's error rather than warning that readers may misunderstand it. |
| ¶16.12 (`u16-12`) | “conceiving, though conceiving of language. This conception…” | “receptive, though receptive to language. This reception…” | Keep the receive/respond action primary; retain conception as a disclosed secondary resonance. |
| ¶17.1 (`u17-1`) | “For conception and spontaneity at once… this word holds also of that conception…” | “For reception and spontaneity together… this word applies also to that reception…” | Continue the approved reception-led family and remove an English-only calque. |
| ¶19.9 (`u19-9`) | “they themselves excited it… the awakening of itself” | “they themselves aroused it… its own awakening” | Remove the modern false friend and keep the `Erweckung` echo audible. |
| ¶21, Müller (between `u21-1` and `u21-4`) | “saw by the nobility with which they sprang away from me, because the man had given them a name” | “saw how nobly they sprang away from me, because the man had given each a name” | The three parallel clauses in Müller's “Huldigung der Tiere” establish noble manner and distributive naming. |
| ¶21.16 (`u21-16`) | “the hundred languages of man” | “the hundreds of human languages” | `hunderten Menschensprachen` is plural and not an exact hundred. |

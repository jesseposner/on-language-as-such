# AUD-P0-001 — Rights-holder and permissions memorandum

**Status:** Research memorandum for internal editorial use only
**Prepared:** 2026-07-12
**Scope:** Likely present ownership and administration of rights in Walter Benjamin's German essay *Über Sprache überhaupt und über die Sprache des Menschen*; permission for a new English translation and worldwide web publication; separate treatment of Edmund Jephcott's existing English translation.
**Not legal advice:** This is a public-records and publisher-practice memorandum. It does not replace a title opinion from qualified counsel or a written representation from a licensor.

## Executive conclusion

1. **The best first clearance target for the German source text and a new English translation is Suhrkamp Verlag GmbH.** Public evidence does not establish that Suhrkamp owns every underlying right. It does establish a continuous publishing relationship with Benjamin's writings, edition-level copyright claims, current publication of the relevant collected editions, and a current rights department that expressly handles English-language foreign/translation rights. Suhrkamp should therefore be asked to confirm, in writing, whether it is the owner, an exclusive licensee, or an administrator acting for another principal, and whether it can grant the requested worldwide rights.

2. **The ultimate underlying owner is not conclusively identified in the public evidence reviewed.** Walter Benjamin's authorial copyright would have passed by succession or otherwise been administered under historical agreements, but no current public estate or agency record found here names the present beneficial owner. Suhrkamp's copyright notices and permissions infrastructure prove a serious rights claim and an administration route; they do not, standing alone, disclose the underlying chain of title. The public WATCH database returned no record for Walter Benjamin.

3. **The Walter Benjamin Archive, the Akademie der Künste, and the Hamburger Stiftung zur Förderung von Wissenschaft und Kultur are custodial/editorial partners, not proven blanket licensors of the published essay.** The Archive says it holds Benjamin's literary estate and supplies the basis for the new critical edition, which Suhrkamp publishes. That custody is important for manuscript provenance and archival reproduction, but ownership of physical manuscripts or digital images is not the same as ownership or administration of all publishing and translation rights. The Archive is an appropriate provenance/escalation contact if Suhrkamp cannot identify the principal or if manuscript images or unpublished variants are to be used.

4. **Rights in Edmund Jephcott's translation are separate.** The 1996 Belknap/Harvard title page identifies “On Language as Such and the Language of Man” as translated by Edmund Jephcott, states that its English translation copyright is © 1978 Harcourt Brace Jovanovich, Inc., and says it was published by arrangement with that company. The same page identifies the source *Gesammelte Schriften* copyrights as Suhrkamp's. This is unusually clear evidence that the source-text/new-translation chain and the Jephcott-expression chain are distinct.

5. **HarperCollins is the strongest current administrator target for Jephcott's 1978 translation.** HarperCollins currently sells *Reflections* under the relevant ISBN; News Corp officially states that Houghton Mifflin Harcourt Books & Media was acquired in 2021 and would be operated by HarperCollins; and HarperCollins's current permissions portal expressly directs requests for Houghton Mifflin Harcourt trade titles through that portal. This supports administration, not a conclusive finding that no reversion, reservation, or third-party interest exists.

6. **Harvard University Press is a secondary, edition-specific contact, not the best first claimant to the Jephcott translation.** HUP owns the 1996 volume's compilation/editorial and other original matter, but its title page attributes Jephcott's translation copyright to Harcourt Brace Jovanovich and says HUP published it by arrangement. HUP's current FAQ likewise tells requesters to follow incorporated third-party material back to its source. Contact HUP if the proposed use includes HUP-specific editorial matter, if HUP's edition is the direct quoted source and its position is wanted, or if HarperCollins refers the request back to HUP.

7. **A worldwide website cannot rely on the essay's public-domain status in Germany alone.** Benjamin died in 1940, so the ordinary German life-plus-seventy term ended after 2010. The site's United States exposure remains materially different. If the essay's 1955 German publication satisfies the URAA restoration conditions, the restored U.S. term would ordinarily continue through 2050. The decisive publication facts — especially any U.S. publication within thirty days — remain unverified. Clearance or a formal U.S. title opinion is still required before treating the worldwide question as closed.

**Recommended operational chain:** open coordinated requests with (a) Suhrkamp's English-rights team for the new translation and (b) Suhrkamp/Frankfurt Rights for full German-text reproduction; ask Suhrkamp to identify its legal capacity and principal. Separately use HarperCollins's permissions portal only for any Jephcott quotations that the project elects to license rather than rely on fair use. Escalate provenance questions to the Walter Benjamin Archive. Have counsel verify the U.S. publication/restoration facts and review the eventual license, especially any proposed CC BY downstream reuse.

## Likely clearance chain

### 1. German source text and new English translation — Suhrkamp first

- Submit the full-German-text/reprint request through [Suhrkamp's Frankfurt Rights portal](https://frankfurtrights.com/portal/suhrkamp-verlag). The official instructions say emailed or mailed reprint requests are not processed.
- Send the new-English-translation inquiry to the English-rights team: Rights Director **Nora Mercurio**, routed through **Sarah Wittenstein**, Assistance to the Rights Director, at `wittenstein@suhrkamp.de`, +49 30 740744-254. State that the coordinated German-text request has also been submitted through Frankfurt Rights.

Ask Suhrkamp to answer each of these title questions expressly:

1. Does Suhrkamp control the right, in the United States and worldwide, to reproduce the full German essay and to authorize a newly made English translation?
2. Is Suhrkamp acting as owner, exclusive licensee, or agent/administrator? If for a principal, what legal person or estate owns the relevant rights, and may Suhrkamp bind that principal for this license?

7. What exact credit, copyright notice, fee, reporting, approval, and integrity conditions would apply?
8. If Suhrkamp cannot grant the rights, will it identify or forward the request to the current owner/administrator?

### 2. Jephcott translation — HarperCollins first if permission is sought

If the editor elects to license some or all Jephcott quotations, submit one request through the [HarperCollins permissions portal](https://permissions.harpercollins.com/) identifying:

- Walter Benjamin, *Reflections: Essays, Aphorisms, Autobiographical Writings*;

Ask HarperCollins to confirm that it currently administers the 1978 HBJ translation copyright and whether any translator/estate reversion or territorial exclusion affects the requested use. Do **not** assume that HarperCollins controls Benjamin's German source-text rights or the right to authorize a wholly new translation merely because it administers Jephcott's version.

## Exact scope to request from Suhrkamp or the confirmed source-rights licensor

- Walter Benjamin, *Über Sprache überhaupt und über die Sprache des Menschen* (written 1916; first published in *Schriften*, Suhrkamp, 1955).

### Rights

- Non-exclusive right to reproduce, publish, distribute, publicly display, and make available the full German text.
- Non-exclusive right to translate the work into English and reproduce, publish, distribute, publicly display, and make available that translation.
- Right to present German and English in parallel and to annotate, link, index, quote, and technically reformat them.

This requires special attention: a CC license from the translator alone cannot authorize exploitation of any still-protected Benjamin material embodied in the translation. The rights holder must either authorize that downstream license or the project must narrow its public license and reserve the underlying work/translation as required.

### Territory, language, term, and channels

- Territory: worldwide, expressly including the United States, Canada, United Kingdom, European Union/EEA, and all other internet-accessible territories.
- Languages: German source and this new English translation only.
- Term: perpetual if available, otherwise the maximum licensable term and at least through expiration of all relevant rights.
- Channels: current project URL `https://jesseposner.github.io/on-language-as-such/`; public repository `https://github.com/jesseposner/on-language-as-such`; web display and repository/download copies necessary to operate and preserve the site.
- Editions: current edition and future revision-numbered versions of the same scholarly project.
- Business model: free public access, no subscription, no advertising, no sale of the text, with attribution and a complete rights notice.
- Exclusions unless separately negotiated: print publication for sale, audiobook, podcast/dramatic reading, film/television, theatrical performance, merchandise, and unrelated commercial reuse.

### Administrative terms to settle

- Exact licensor name and legal capacity.
- Warrant that the licensor controls the granted rights and list any exclusions or third-party consents.
- Required copyright/permission credit and placement.
- Fee, royalty, reporting, approval, audit, renewal, and termination terms.
- Whether editorial corrections, translation revisions, or future editions need approval.
- Whether the licensor requires geoblocking before or after a stated date.
- Cure period and notice method before takedown/termination, if negotiable.
- Whether existing publication before the grant is released, ratified, or expressly excluded.

## Contradictions and cautions

1. **Suhrkamp's “©” notices versus ownership.** The 1955/1977/current edition notices and translation reservation are substantial evidence of claimed rights and administration. They may combine underlying rights, exclusive licenses, editorial copyrights, and publisher production rights. They do not identify the beneficial owner or prove the scope of a 2026 U.S. grant.

2. **German public domain versus current permissions machinery.** The ordinary German authorial term expired after 2010, yet Suhrkamp still publishes and licenses modern editions. This is not necessarily contradictory: edition-specific material, contracts, other territories, and U.S. restored rights can remain relevant. A German public-domain conclusion does not answer a worldwide-web request.

3. **Archive custody versus copyright.** The Walter Benjamin Archive “holds the literary estate,” and the Hamburger Stiftung sponsors the archive/critical edition. Those facts can mean physical custody, ownership of objects, editorial authority, or some rights interests. They do not publicly establish that either institution can license all published text and translation rights. Conversely, Suhrkamp's role does not prove it owns the manuscripts or facsimile rights.

4. **HUP's overall © 1996 notice versus the Jephcott attribution.** The specific 1978 HBJ attribution controls the analysis of the incorporated Jephcott translation more directly than the volume-level HUP notice. HUP's FAQ reinforces that third-party-source distinction.

5. **Multiple publishers of *Reflections*.** Harcourt/HBJ, Schocken, Houghton Mifflin Harcourt/Mariner, HUP, and now HarperCollins appear in the publication history. Publication by several houses can reflect licensed editions. The clearest direct chain for present intake is HBJ → HMH/Mariner backlist → HarperCollins, corroborated by the current Harper product and the official HMH acquisition notice; title-specific confirmation remains necessary.

6. **No WATCH record.** This lowers neither the risk nor the need to investigate. WATCH is a finding aid, not a registry of all protected authors and estates.

7. **First publication and URAA remain factual hinges.** The edition's bibliography says 1955, and the 1977 critical notes state that the essay remained unpublished during Benjamin's lifetime. The reviewed sources did not conclusively establish whether an authorized U.S. publication occurred within thirty days of the 1955 German publication. Do not convert the “likely through 2050” hypothesis into an unconditional legal statement until this is checked.

8. **Fair use of Jephcott quotations is separate from source clearance.** A license for the Benjamin source/new translation does not automatically license Jephcott wording. A license for Jephcott quotations does not authorize the new translation. Fair use may make a Jephcott permission unnecessary, but that decision belongs in the legal/editorial risk analysis, not in the source-rights request.

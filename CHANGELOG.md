# Revision History

This file records published, reader-visible changes to the living edition. The editorial protocol is defined in [EDITORIAL.md](EDITORIAL.md); proposed and unpublished work is not represented as part of the public edition.

Versions use calendar dates. Paragraph and sentence identifiers refer to the edition's stable internal anchors.

## 2026.07.12.2

### Edition

- Published the approved translation-policy, <em>Sache</em>/<em>Materie</em>,
  commentary, and living-edition navigation revisions recorded in
  AUD-P1-001 and its focused subdecisions.

### Translation policy

- Retained “quintessence” for <em>geistiges Wesen</em> and “quintessential”
  in its related adjective slots.
- Expanded the translator’s-note explanation to disclose the lost
  <em>Geist</em> root, distinguish lexical evidence from interpretation, and
  identify the Hamlet echo as an English resonance rather than a source.

### Translation

- ¶7.5: changed “he imparts some matter to other men” to “he imparts
  factual subject matter to other men,” following the factual/content sense
  of <em>eine Sache</em> and the canonical English as a control witness.
- ¶7.7: changed “its object the matter” to “its object the subject matter,”
  preserving the means–object–addressee triad without implying material
  substance.
- ¶16.9: changed “the word relates to the matter accidentally” to “the word
  relates to its object accidentally,” matching the local word–referent
  relation.
- ¶16.9: changed “the essence of the matter” to “the essence of the thing.”
- ¶16.9: changed “the matter in itself” to “the thing in itself,” preserving
  the formal force of <em>die Sache an sich</em>.
- ¶16.12: changed “This knowledge of the matter” to “This knowledge of the
  thing.”
- ¶16.12: changed “the name that man gives to the matter” to “the name that
  man gives to the thing,” correcting the received English’s “language” slip
  against German <em>der Sache</em>.
- Reserved unqualified “matter” for the three genuine <em>Materie</em> sites in
  ¶12, ¶14, and ¶18; synchronized the translator’s note and repeated
  commentary quotations with the seven approved readings.

### Commentary

- Recalibrated the apparatus, ¶1 commentary, and epilogue so the German
  alchemical nodes are distinguished from the constellation intensified by
  “quintessence” in English.
- Normalized unmarked mind/spirit paraphrases to the edition’s declared
  quintessence and spirit policies.

### Interface

- Added a visible “Living edition · Revision history” link beneath the
  byline, making the edition history discoverable without adding another
  control to the crowded mobile toolbar.

### Scope

- No German reading changed in this edition.

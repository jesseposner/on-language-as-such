# German collation record

- **Collated:** 2026-07-12
- **Base:** Walter Benjamin, “Über Sprache überhaupt und über die Sprache des
  Menschen,” *Gesammelte Schriften* II.1, pp. 140–157
- **Critical report:** *Gesammelte Schriften* II.3, pp. 931–936
- **Object compared:** published Edition 2026.07.12.4 German in `index.html`

This record distinguishes the reported T1–T4 transmission, the printed GS
critical constitution, the former textlog-derived edition (ED), and the adopted
critical reading (READ). Page-image inspection, not OCR alone, governs every GS
reading below.

## Witness genealogy

- **T1:** typescript with Benjamin’s handwritten corrections; GS
  `Druckvorlage`.
- **T2–T4:** three reported typescript copies. Direct facsimiles were not
  inspected for this edition.
- **GS:** T1-based critical text with three expressly disclosed conjectures.
- **TL / PG:** textlog and Projekt Gutenberg share a diagnostic corruption
  cluster and are treated as one dependent electronic branch.
- **RL:** Fred Lönker’s Reclam 2019 critical reading edition, checked through
  its official publisher sample.
- **EJ:** Edmund Jephcott’s canonical English translation, used as an editorial
  control rather than a German witness.

The 1955 *Schriften* II text pages and T1–T4 facsimiles remain desirable future
comparators, not prerequisites for the present reading.

## Adopted German emphasis

The following is an exhaustive logical-range inventory of GS emphasis. Line
wrapping in print and PDF XML can split one range into several fragments.

- **¶2:** `Sprache`; `durch`; `sich`; `Λόγος`.
- **¶3:** first `in` / `durch`; second `durch`; `sofern`; suffix `-bar`;
  final `mitteilbar`.
- **¶4:** `was`; `Jede Sprache teilt sich selbst mit`; `mitteilbar`;
  `Das sprachliche Wesen der Dinge ist ihre Sprache`; `ist`; `erscheint`;
  suffix `-bare`; `an`; two local `in` contrasts; stem `Unmittel`; final
  `durch` / `in` contrast.
- **¶5:** `in`; `benennt`; `benennende`; `Das sprachliche Wesen des Menschen
  ist also, daß er die Dinge benennt`.
- **¶6:** `er`; `sie` in `Aber er benennt sie`.
- **¶7:** two `durch` instances; opposed `in`; `im Namen teilt das geistige
  Wesen des Menschen sich Gott mit`.
- **¶8:** `durch`; `in`; `die`; `daß die Sprache schlechthin`; `heißen`.
- **¶9:** `Der Mensch allein hat die nach Universalität und Intensität
  vollkommene Sprache`.
- **¶10:** `zunächst`; `in`; the proposition beginning `Einen Inhalt der
  Sprache gibt es nicht` and ending `Mitteilbarkeit schlechthin mit`.
- **¶11:** `Sprache, die Mutter`; `Offenbarung` in the Hamann quotation.
- **¶13:** `zunächst`; `erzählt`; `Gabe`.
- **¶15:** `ihm`.
- **¶16:** `aber` in the Genesis quotation; `schöpferischen`; `bloße`.
- **¶19:** `die` in `in die Sprache`; `menschlichen`; `etwas`; `bloßen`.
- **¶20:** `Zeichen`.
- **¶21:** `Menschen`; `etwas`.
- **¶¶1, 12, 14, 17–18, 22–26:** no additional GS emphasis.

## Translation emphasis decisions

The English carries source emphasis where lexical correspondence is direct.
For German suffix emphasis `-bar`, English uses `-able`; for the German stem
`Unmittel`, English emphasizes the full functional counterpart “immediacy.” At
the ¶8 emphasized article `die`, English emphasizes “language itself,” since
English has no corresponding article in the adopted syntax. These are
translation decisions, not claims of typographic identity.
